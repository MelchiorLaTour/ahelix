# Relationship Logic Reference

## What 3-6-9 feels like in practice

### 3-Relationship (Adjacent strand — partial agreement)
These agents share a common axis but approach it from different angles.
Think of two people who read the same book and came away with different lessons.

**Tone**: Collegial but probing. "I see where you're coming from, but..."
**Challenge style**: Gentle pressure on an assumption they share
**Example**: A techno-optimist (Agent 1) and a pragmatist (Agent 4) both believe
technology can solve problems — but disagree on timeline and who controls it.

### 6-Relationship (Across the helix — maximum tension)
These agents occupy genuinely opposing positions. Not strawmen — both are
fully committed to incompatible worldviews.

**Tone**: Direct, sharp, respectful but unsparing. "No — here's why that's wrong."
**Challenge style**: Hard question that exposes a logical gap or value conflict
**Example**: The Moralist (Agent 2) and the Pragmatist (Agent 5) will clash on
whether good outcomes justify compromised means.

The 6-relationship is the engine of the debate. Without it, deliberation
collapses into consensus. Guard it carefully — do not soften 6-rel exchanges.

### 9-Relationship (Full coil — meta-convergence)
These agents have traveled the full arc of the helix and can now see each other
from above. They don't agree — but they can frame each other's position inside
a larger structure.

**Tone**: Philosophical, wide-angle. "What if we're both describing the same thing
from different altitudes?"
**Challenge style**: A question that reframes the debate itself, not the positions
**Example**: The Empiricist (Agent 1) and the Systems Thinker (Agent 7) might
discover they're both trying to find invariants — one in data, one in structure.

The 9-synthesis should feel surprising. If it feels obvious, go deeper.

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **False balance**: Don't make every agent equally "reasonable" in tone.
  Let the Contrarian be sharp. Let the Moralist be uncompromising.
- **Premature synthesis**: Don't let agents find middle ground in Round 1.
  Tension must build before it resolves.
- **Weak 6-relationships**: The cross-helix clash is the most important exchange.
  Don't soften it for comfort.
- **Vague 9-synthesis**: "Both sides have a point" is not synthesis.
  The 9-synthesis must name something that wasn't visible before the debate.
